Victoria, British Columbia lacks an accessible, interdisciplinary "third space" for hands-on learning. Crossroads bridges this gap, providing community members of all ages with tools, mentorship, and opportunities outside restrictive institutions.

Explore the Proposal

The Need & The Solution

This section outlines the core problem facing STEM education in Greater Victoria and how the Crossroads model utilizes experiential learning and spatial design to overcome these barriers.

⚠️ The Problem

Traditional education relies on passive instruction. Access to industrial tools is locked behind high university tuition or restrictive maker-clubs.

📖 The Theory

Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle proves that concrete experience yields higher retention. The "Third Space" theory anchors community life.

💡 The Solution

A true drop-in center spanning 15 disciplines. No application process. Sliding-scale fees. Mentorship embedded into every interaction.

⚠️
Current State

The Infrastructure Gap

The founder discovered firsthand that many young people lack environments that nurture curiosity. In Canada, rural areas lack dedicated facilities, while urban centers concentrate STEM access within universities imposing massive barriers.

Current makerspaces in Victoria function more as hobbyist clubs with restricted schedules rather than open, beginner-friendly educational centers.
