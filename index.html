Project Overview

The Crossroads Community STEM Centre is a proposed nonprofit makerspace in Langford, BC, designed to address the 78% access barrier to STEM tools and education identified in community survey research (n=54). Feasibility Assessment Framework

Six dimensions of feasibility were evaluated using the framework developed in the Crossroads Feasibility Study (2026). Each dimension integrates quantitative data from the financial model with qualitative assessments from community engagement.

Market Feasibility

Strong

78% barrier identification rate, 65% facility interest, and 63,120 service area population support sustained demand. No direct competitor within 30 km.

Key Data Points
  • Population growth: 3.95% year-over-year (Vancouver Island's fastest-growing municipality)
  • Survey sample: 54 respondents, Feb 2026
  • Fee preference: 61% preferred $25-$50/month range
  • Peak demand: Evenings (68%), weekends (59%)
  • Access gap: UVic and Camosun restrict lab access to enrolled students; Victoria Makerspace uses application-based membership with limited drop-in
  • Demand by discipline: 3D Printing/Fabrication (72%), Software Dev (58%), Robotics (54%), Creative Design (41%)

Financial Feasibility

Viable

Break-even at ~147 members (lean model) against a target of 200. Monthly surplus of $3,167 at baseline. Seven diversified revenue streams reduce single-source dependency.

Key Data Points
  • Lean startup: $476,100 (adjusted +32.2% from original $359,950 estimate)
  • Annual operating: $310,000 (lean) / $450,000 (expanded)
  • Revenue target: $29,000/month from 7 diversified streams
  • Single-source cap: No single funding source exceeds 30% of total revenue
  • Break-even timeline: Month 8 (expected scenario)
  • Key cost adjustments: Lease +25% to $28-$30/sqft gross, Personnel +50% to $165,000, Insurance +40% to $28,000
  • Safety margin: 26% below target enrollment (147 vs 200 members)

Technical Feasibility

Moderate

Equipment requirements are well-defined with established supply chains. Safety protocols for high-risk machinery require certified instructors (2 FTE minimum).

Key Data Points
  • Core equipment: FDM 3D printers, 40W CO2 laser cutter, benchtop CNC routers, electronics testing hardware
  • Electrical: 200-amp service required for fabrication areas
  • Facility options: McCallum Road and West Shore Parkway commercial zones in Langford
  • Lean model: 4,000 sqft with core equipment clusters
  • Expanded model: 6,000 sqft adding larger CNC router and enterprise digital infrastructure
  • Fallback: Pop-Up model using mobile workshops in rented community center space
  • Compliance: Accessible Canada Act, industrial ventilation for fabrication areas

Organizational Feasibility

Strong

Nonprofit society structure aligns with community mission. SD62 partnership provides facility access pathways. Advisory board model reduces governance overhead.

Key Data Points
  • Staffing: 2 full-time safety instructors working staggered shifts
  • Access hours: 63 staffed hours/week (lean), 73 total access hours
  • Volunteer corps: 15-25 projected active volunteer mentors
  • Partnerships: School Districts 61, 62, and 63; Camosun College CTAC (potential $25,000 in equipment donations)
  • Disciplines: Robotics/Electronics, Digital Fabrication, Software Development, Creative/Applied Design
  • Revenue mix: Single-source dependency capped at 30%; sliding-scale memberships, drop-in passes, workshop fees

Legal & Regulatory

Manageable

BC Societies Act incorporation is straightforward. High-risk insurance (CGL) is the primary cost driver at $28K-$49K. WorkSafeBC compliance for volunteer coverage is addressable.

Key Data Points
  • Incorporation: Nonprofit society under BC Societies Act (required for public trust and grant eligibility)
  • Insurance: CGL insurance with specialized riders for open-access high-risk machinery (CO2 lasers, CNC mills)
  • Insurance cost: $28,000 (lean) to $49,000 (expanded), originally omitted from early estimates
  • Employer obligations: WorkSafeBC premiums, CPP, and EI contributions mandatory
  • Safety: Mandatory safety orientation; dual-supervisor coverage during high-risk equipment operation
  • Political climate: Strong political support for workforce readiness programs in the West Shore

Scheduling Feasibility

At Risk

63 staffed hours per week across 7 days (9 hrs/day) requires tight scheduling. Equipment maintenance windows reduce productive hours. Peak demand (evenings 68%, weekends 59%) concentrates load.

Key Data Points
  • Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Nonprofit incorporation and lease negotiation
  • Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Facility renovation and equipment commissioning
  • Phase 3 (Months 7-9): Soft launch, pilot workshops, protocol testing
  • Phase 4 (Months 10-12): Full public launch and comprehensive evaluation
  • Staffing constraint: 2 FTE instructors covering 63 hours/week requires staggered shifts with overlapping peak-hour coverage
  • Risk: Worst-case membership stalls below 120 users, triggering Pop-Up Fallback contingency

Conclusion & Recommendations

The Crossroads Community STEM Centre demonstrates conditional financial feasibility under the lean model, achieving a $3,167 monthly surplus at the baseline membership target of 200. The break-even threshold of approximately 147 members provides a 26% safety margin below the target enrollment.

Key risk factors include:

  • Membership acquisition rate during the first 6–12 months (ramp-up period)
  • Grant and sponsorship revenue stability (30% of non-membership revenue)
  • Insurance cost volatility for high-risk makerspace operations
  • Scheduling constraints at 63 staffed hours per week with concentrated peak demand

The expanded model ($738,300 startup) is viable only with institutional co-investment or phased equipment acquisition. Recommendation is to proceed with the lean model, scaling to expanded capacity once membership exceeds 200 sustained members for 6+ consecutive months.

Next steps: Formal nonprofit incorporation, insurance quotes from 3+ brokers, SD62 facility partnership MOU, and grant applications targeting NSERC PromoScience and BC Community Gaming.
